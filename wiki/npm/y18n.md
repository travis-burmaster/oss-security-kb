## Security Posture Notes

- `y18n` has a **single currently published package advisory** in this pass, centered on prototype pollution rather than a broader vulnerability cluster.
- The public remediation path is easy to trace: issue `#96` is titled `Prototype pollution`, PR `#108` is titled `fix: address prototype pollution issue`, and public commits `90401ee` and `a9ac604` carry the same remediation theme.
- The package's install base is enormous (**~124.9M weekly downloads** in this review window), so even a single older utility-package flaw can retain significant downstream blast radius.
- The advisory is notable because fixes were coordinated across multiple major lines (`3.x`, `4.x`, and `5.x`) rather than only the newest release family.
- No repository-root `SECURITY.md` was confirmed in this pass, so disclosure guidance remains less explicit than the package's ecosystem reach would ideally warrant.
- No additional package-scoped OSV records were surfaced for `y18n` in this review beyond `GHSA-c4w7-xm78-47vh`.

## Recommendations for Developers

1. **Upgrade to a fixed line**: `>=3.2.2`, `>=4.0.1`, or `>=5.0.5`, depending on your supported major version.
2. **Scan lockfiles for stale transitive copies**, since `y18n` often arrives through CLI and argument-parsing stacks rather than as a conscious direct dependency.
3. **Watch small merge / object-update helpers closely** in localization and configuration tooling, because prototype-pollution bugs often hide in low-level utility paths.

## Dependencies of Note

- Commonly appears transitively in command-line and argument-parsing ecosystems around `yargs` rather than as an application-level dependency chosen for security-sensitive logic.
- High-download localization helpers can still matter in CI, developer tooling, and server-side automation where polluted objects may influence control flow.
